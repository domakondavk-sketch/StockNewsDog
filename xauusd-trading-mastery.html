Gold and the US Dollar typically have a strong inverse correlation. When USD strengthens, gold tends to fall, and vice versa.

DXY Rising üî¥ Bearish for Gold
DXY Falling üü¢ Bullish for Gold
Monitor: Dollar Index (DXY), EUR/USD (inverse correlation with DXY), and US Treasury yields
üìâInterest Rates & Fed Policy
  • Rate Hikes - Bearish for gold (makes non-yielding gold less attractive)
  • Rate Cuts - Bullish for gold (reduces opportunity cost)
  • Dovish Fed - Weakens USD, supports gold prices
  • Hawkish Fed - Strengthens USD, pressures gold
Critical Events: FOMC meetings, Fed Chair speeches, CPI/PPI data, NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls), and Fed minutes releases
‚ö°Geopolitical & Risk Events

Gold is a safe-haven asset that typically rallies during uncertainty:

  • Wars and military conflicts
  • Political instability or elections
  • Banking crises or financial system stress
  • Global pandemic or health crises
  • Trade wars and tariff disputes
  • Debt ceiling concerns
Risk-On vs Risk-Off: Monitor VIX (fear index) and stock markets. When stocks fall sharply, gold often rallies.

üíéPosition Sizing Rules
The 1-2% Rule

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on any single trade.

Example: $10,000 account ‚Üí Maximum risk per trade = $100-200

Conservative 0.5-1% per trade
Moderate 1-2% per trade
Aggressive (NOT recommended) 2-3% per trade
Warning: Risking more than 2% per trade significantly increases risk of ruin. Professionals rarely exceed 1%.

üõëStop Loss Strategies
  • ATR-based stops - 1.5-2x ATR(14) for volatility-adjusted stops
  • Structure-based stops - Beyond swing high/low or key support/resistance
  • Percentage stops - Fixed % from entry (0.5-1% of gold price)
  • Time-based stops - Exit if setup doesn't work within expected timeframe
Never Move Stop Loss Against You! Once set, only move it to lock in profits (trailing stop).
Gold-Specific: Typical stop loss range is 20-50 pips depending on timeframe and volatility.
üìäDrawdown Management
Maximum Daily Loss 3-5% of account
Maximum Weekly Loss 10% of account
Maximum Monthly Loss 15-20% of account
Recovery Rule

If you hit your daily loss limit, STOP trading immediately. Take the rest of the day off and analyze what went wrong.

After a 10% drawdown, reduce position size by 50% until you recover to within 5% of peak equity.

‚öñÔ∏èLeverage Guidelines

Gold is highly volatile. Use leverage conservatively:

Beginner 1:10 to 1:20
Intermediate 1:20 to 1:50
Advanced 1:50 to 1:100
Critical: Higher leverage = faster account blowout. Most professionals use 1:20-1:50 maximum. Never use maximum available leverage!
üìàTrade Management
Partial Profit Taking

At 1R (1:1): Close 50% of position, move SL to breakeven

At 2R (1:2): Close another 25%, trail SL to 1R

At 3R+ (1:3+): Let remaining 25% run with trailing stop

Scaling Out Benefits: Locks in profits, reduces stress, and allows winners to run while protecting capital.
Trailing Stops: Use ATR-based trailing stops (1.5x ATR) or trail below swing lows/highs on lower timeframes.

üß†Mental Game Mastery
  • Emotional Discipline - Never trade while angry, frustrated, or euphoric
  • Accept Losses - Losses are business expenses, not personal failures
  • Focus on Process - Control what you can control (strategy, risk) not outcomes
  • Patience - Wait for A+ setups only, quality over quantity
  • Detachment - Once trade is placed, let it work. Don't micromanage
Winning Mindset: Think in probabilities, not certainties. Every trade is just one in a series of hundreds.
‚ö†Ô∏èCommon Psychological Traps
Revenge Trading - After a loss, immediately taking another trade to "win it back." Recipe for disaster.
Overtrading - Taking too many trades due to boredom or trying to force profits. Quality beats quantity.
FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) - Jumping into trades late because price is moving. Usually results in buying tops/selling bottoms.
Moving Stop Losses - Widening stops when price approaches them. This destroys risk management.
Overconfidence After Wins - Increasing risk after winning streak. Markets punish overconfidence.
